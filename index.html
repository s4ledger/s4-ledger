Frequently Asked Questions

What is Solus Protocol? +
Solus Protocol is a decentralized infrastructure for medical data integrity, using SHA-256 hash anchoring on the XRP Ledger. It allows healthcare providers to verify record authenticity without exposing patient data.
What is the $SLS token? +
$SLS is the native utility token of the Solus Protocol, live on the XRP Ledger mainnet. It's used for transaction fees, usage rebates, data contribution rewards, and governance voting.
Is Solus Protocol HIPAA compliant? +
Solus Protocol is designed with HIPAA compliance in mind. Since no Protected Health Information (PHI) is ever stored on-chain‚Äîonly cryptographic hashes‚Äîthe system inherently protects patient privacy.
Why use the XRP Ledger? +
XRPL offers near-instant finality (3-5 seconds), extremely low transaction costs (~$0.001), proven reliability since 2012, and carbon-neutral operation‚Äîmaking it ideal for healthcare applications.
How is my patient data protected? +
Your actual medical records never leave your healthcare provider's systems. Only a one-way cryptographic hash is generated and stored on XRPL. This hash cannot be reversed to reveal your data.
How is Solus Protocol funded? +
Solus Protocol is currently self-funded and bootstrapped. We are actively seeking strategic investors and grant funding to accelerate development and pilot partnerships.
What is the total supply of $SLS tokens? +
The total supply and tokenomics details are available in our whitepaper. $SLS is designed with a sustainable economic model to support long-term protocol growth.
What makes Solus different from other healthcare blockchain projects? +
Solus Protocol focuses specifically on data integrity verification rather than data storage. Our zero-knowledge approach, combined with XRPL's speed and cost efficiency, provides a practical solution that can be implemented today.
