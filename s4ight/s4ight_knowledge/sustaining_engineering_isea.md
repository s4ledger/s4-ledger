# Sustaining Engineering (ISEA Model)

For PMS surface and auxiliary platforms, sustaining engineering is
typically delivered through the In-Service Engineering Agent (ISEA)
model — usually a NSWC field activity (e.g., NSWC Philadelphia for
auxiliary machinery, NSWC Carderock for hydrodynamics, NSWC Dahlgren
for combat systems where applicable) and / or the cognizant NAVSEA
05/04 organization for the platform.

## Scope of Sustaining Engineering

- Configuration & technical data management (TDP currency).
- Deficiency reporting and resolution (DRs).
- DMSMS / obsolescence engineering changes.
- Engineering change proposals (ECPs) and class changes.
- Sustaining cybersecurity (software/firmware patching).
- Fleet support — RFAs, hotline calls, on-site emergent assists.

## Funding Profile

Sustaining engineering is OMN-funded (operations and maintenance) for
in-service systems and RDT&E/PMC during development. Profile errors are
the single most common reason sustaining engineering capacity falls
behind demand at IOC.

## Key Documents / Hooks

- **In-Service Engineering Plan (ISEP)** or equivalent for the platform.
- **LCSP §11 / IPS Element 11** — Sustaining Engineering.
- **ISEA SOW** with the field activity (per-platform).
- **DR tracking system** (e.g., NDRS, NSCS, platform-specific).

## Common Failure Modes

- Funding cliff at IOC — sustaining engineering halved when demand peaks.
- ISEA SOW lags configuration changes; rework loops.
- DR backlog grows; mean closure age exceeds 180 days.
- No metric reporting on sustaining engineering load to PM.

## S4ight Guidance

- For any sustaining engineering question, ask: which platform / class,
  who is the cognizant ISEA, current OMN profile vs. demand signal.
- Tie sustaining engineering risks to LCSP §11 and the IMS.
- Recommend monthly DR-aging and capacity-burndown reporting to PM.
