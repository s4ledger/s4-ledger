# Integrated Logistics Assessment (ILA)

## Purpose
The ILA is a structured assessment of a program's product support readiness,
performed prior to major milestones (MS B, MS C, FRP) and at key sustainment
decision points. It evaluates maturity of the 12 IPS elements and the LCSP.

## Triggering Events
- MS B, MS C, FRP/IOC decisions.
- Major capability upgrade or sustainment re-baselining.
- ASN(RD&A) or PEO-directed health checks.

## Standard Process
1. **Pre-ILA** — PMO assembles evidence pack (LCSP, ILS plans, provisioning
   status, training plan, EVMS data, risk register).
2. **On-site ILA Review** — independent team scores each IPS element on a
   defined maturity scale (typically Green/Yellow/Red with criteria).
3. **Findings & Recommendations** — written report; identifies critical gaps.
4. **Corrective Action Plan (CAP)** — PMO responds with owners and dates.
5. **Closure** — verified by ILA team or stakeholder governance.

## Common Findings (recurring patterns)
- Provisioning data incomplete or stale (Supply Support).
- TDP gaps that block sustainment (Technical Data + CM).
- Training products lag fielding schedule.
- Support equipment requirements untraceable to maintenance concept.
- Sustaining engineering funding profile not credible.
- LCSP not aligned with current TDP / CM baseline.

## S4ight Guidance
- Always treat the ILA as a forcing function — it is the most actionable
  external view of program supportability.
- For each Red/Yellow finding, S4ight should produce a draft CAP entry with:
  owner, due date, success criteria, and the IMS/EVMS hook.
- Map findings back to the LCSP section that needs to be updated.
