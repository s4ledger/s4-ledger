# Life Cycle Sustainment Plan (LCSP)

## Purpose
The LCSP is the Product Support Manager's (PSM) authoritative plan for how the
weapon system or platform will be sustained across its life. It is a statutory
and regulatory milestone deliverable governed by DoDI 5000.91 and supporting
DoD/DoN guidance.

## Required at Major Milestones
- **Milestone A** — initial draft, defines sustainment strategy and approach.
- **Milestone B** — refined; supports the Acquisition Strategy and contract
  awards.
- **Milestone C** — mature; ready to execute initial fielding.
- **FRP / IOC** — execution version, updated for sustainment ramp.
- **Sustainment reviews** — refreshed periodically post-IOC.

## Core Sections (typical)
1. Sustainment Strategy & Performance Outcomes (RAM-C, Ao, MTBF, MTBR).
2. Product Support Strategy (organic / contractor / hybrid; PBL constructs).
3. Product Support Package — coverage of all 12 IPS elements.
4. Funding Profile (O&S costs by year and appropriation).
5. Sustainment Risks (linked to the program risk register).
6. Performance Measurement, Reporting, and Reviews.
7. Configuration & Technical Data Management approach.
8. Sustainment Schedule & Key Decision Points.

## Common Pitfalls
- LCSP not reconciled with TDP baseline or current CM decisions.
- Funding profile misaligned with actual demand signal.
- IPS element coverage gaps (e.g., training or support equipment treated
  superficially).
- No clear tie from LCSP risks to the IMS critical path or EVMS.

## S4ight Guidance
When a user asks about the LCSP:
- Ask which milestone and which IPS elements are in scope.
- Recommend the PSM lead a cross-functional LCSP working group with ILS,
  acquisition, engineering, contracts, and the ISEA.
- Always tie LCSP updates to the next decision event (gate review,
  sustainment review, contract option).
