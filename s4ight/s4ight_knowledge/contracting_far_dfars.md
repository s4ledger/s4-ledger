# Contracting Hooks — FAR / DFARS Snapshot for Sustainment

S4ight is not legal counsel — but it must understand which FAR/DFARS
levers PMS programs commonly use, so its recommendations stay credible.

## Sustainment-Relevant Clauses (high level)

- **FAR 52.246 series** — Inspection / quality clauses.
- **FAR 52.227 series** — Patent rights & technical data.
- **DFARS 252.227-7013 / 7014 / 7015** — Rights in technical data &
  computer software; foundational for TDP rights.
- **DFARS 252.246-7008** — Sources of supply.
- **DFARS 252.227-7017** — Identification & assertion of restrictions
  on technical data.
- **DFARS 252.246-7007 / 7008** — Counterfeit electronic part avoidance.

## Vehicle Patterns

- **Performance-Based Logistics (PBL)** — outcome-based sustainment; common
  on auxiliary classes for material readiness or Ao targets.
- **IDIQ / MAC** — task-order driven; supports rapid sustainment work via
  ISEA-aligned task orders.
- **OTA** — used selectively for prototyping or middle-tier acquisition;
  not the default for steady-state sustainment.

## Common Failure Modes

- TDP delivered without sufficient rights → can't compete sustainment.
- DRL items duplicated across CDRLs → contractor rework / disputes.
- PBL outcome metrics not measurable → "PBL in name only".

## S4ight Guidance

- For any contracting question, ask: contract vehicle, period of
  performance, sustainment outcome (PBL vs. T&M vs. FFP), and the data
  rights situation.
- Always recommend KO / KTR coordination — S4ight never speaks for the KO.
- Tie contract terms back to LCSP §3 (Product Support Package) and §4
  (Funding Profile).
