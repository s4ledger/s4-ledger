## Mission Focus
PMS 325 (Strategic and Theater Sealift Program Office) manages design,
acquisition, and life-cycle sustainment for Navy sealift platforms,
including auxiliary/replenishment ships (e.g., T-AO 205 John Lewis-class
fleet replenishment oilers, T-AKE, T-ESD/ESB, and related auxiliaries).

## Typical Platforms / Examples
- T-AO 205 John Lewis-class fleet replenishment oilers.
- T-AKE Lewis and Clark-class dry cargo/ammunition ships.
- T-ESD / T-ESB expeditionary sea base / transfer dock ships.

## Sustainment Reality
- Long-life platforms with diverse legacy and new-build hulls.
- Heavy reliance on Military Sealift Command (MSC) operational profile.
- Sustainment partnership with NAVSUP WSS, NSLC, and platform ISEAs.
- Significant focus on availability, depot maintenance cycles, and
  long-lead provisioning.

## Typical S4ight Use Cases
- Pre-MS C ILA preparation for new-build classes.
- LCSP refresh tied to next sustainment review.
- Provisioning health on long-lead items for outfitting periods.
- Sustaining engineering capacity planning across multiple hulls.
- EVMS / IMS health on a specific contract or sustainment task order.
