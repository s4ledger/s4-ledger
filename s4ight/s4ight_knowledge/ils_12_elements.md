# 12 Elements of Integrated Logistics Support (ILS)

ILS is the unified, DoD-standard approach for designing, fielding, and sustaining
weapon systems and supporting equipment. The 12 elements work together across
the lifecycle to deliver materiel readiness at the lowest total ownership cost.

## The 12 Elements

1. **Maintenance Planning & Management** — Define how the system will be
   maintained at organizational, intermediate, and depot levels. Drives MRC
   development, LORA, RCM analysis, and the maintenance concept in the LCSP.

2. **Supply Support** — Provisioning of repair parts, consumables, and
   support items. Includes initial provisioning, allowance lists, NSN
   assignment, and replenishment planning with NAVSUP/NSLC.

3. **Support Equipment** — Tools, test equipment, calibration assets needed
   to operate and maintain the system. Identify common vs. peculiar; minimize
   peculiar SE.

4. **Manpower & Personnel** — Required quantities and skill mixes (NEC, NOBC,
   ratings) to operate and maintain. Feeds MPT analysis.

5. **Training & Training Support** — Operator and maintainer training products
   (courseware, simulators, OJT). Tied to manpower/personnel decisions.

6. **Technical Data** — All technical publications, drawings, IETMs, software
   documentation. Managed under Configuration Management; the TDP must be
   complete enough to support provisioning and sustainment.

7. **Computer Resources Support** — Sustainment of mission-critical software,
   firmware, computing hardware; includes cybersecurity sustainment.

8. **Facilities & Infrastructure** — Shore-side and shipboard facilities
   required to support operations, maintenance, training, and storage.

9. **Packaging, Handling, Storage & Transportation (PHS&T)** — How items move
   and are stored from contractor to point of use, including hazmat and
   special handling.

10. **Design Interface** — Two-way influence between supportability and
    system design (reliability, maintainability, testability, HSI).

11. **Sustaining Engineering** — Ongoing engineering after fielding:
    obsolescence, diminishing manufacturing sources, deficiency resolution,
    in-service engineering agent (ISEA) support.

12. **Product Support Management** — The integrating function. The Product
    Support Manager (PSM) owns the LCSP and the 12 IPS elements (DoD now
    uses 12 IPS elements; many programs still call them ILS).

## Common Failure Modes
- Provisioning data immature at MS C → fielded gaps.
- LCSP not synchronized with TDP and CM baseline.
- Training & Manpower decisions made after design lock → late re-work.
- Sustaining Engineering under-funded once program transitions to sustainment.

## Key Hooks for S4ight
- For any ILS question, identify which of the 12 elements it primarily touches.
- Always tie analysis back to the LCSP and to risks in the IMS/EVMS.
- For PMS 300/325/385, link to NAVSUP WSS, NSLC Mechanicsburg, and the
  cognizant ISEA for the platform.
